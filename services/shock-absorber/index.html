Why Shock Absorbers Matter

Shock absorbers (or struts) control your vehicle's suspension movement, keeping tires in contact with the road and providing a smooth, controlled ride. Worn shocks reduce handling, increase stopping distance, cause uneven tire wear, and make your vehicle feel unstable. Replacing worn shocks dramatically improves safety, comfort, and vehicle control.

Replacement Interval

Typical Lifespan: 50,000-100,000 miles depending on driving conditions
Inspect At: Every tire rotation or if you notice any symptoms

Shocks wear gradually, so you may not notice the degradation until they're replaced and you feel the difference.

Signs You Need New Shocks

Excessive Bouncing

Vehicle continues bouncing after hitting a bump, or nose dives when braking

Poor Handling

Vehicle sways, leans excessively in turns, or feels unstable at highway speeds

Fluid Leaks

Visible oil on shock body or dripping fluid indicates seal failure

Uneven Tire Wear

Cupping or scalloping pattern on tire tread from bouncing

Longer Stopping Distance

Vehicle takes longer to stop or feels less stable during braking
