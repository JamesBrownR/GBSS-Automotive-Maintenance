What Does a Thermostat Do?

Your engine's thermostat is a temperature-controlled valve that regulates coolant flow between the engine and radiator. It keeps the engine closed off from the cooling system until it reaches optimal operating temperature, then opens to allow coolant circulation. A properly functioning thermostat ensures your engine warms up quickly, runs efficiently, and maintains the correct temperature under all conditions.

Common Thermostat Problems

Stuck Closed: Engine overheats because coolant can't circulate

Stuck Open: Engine runs too cold, poor fuel economy, no heat from heater

Intermittent: Temperature fluctuates erratically while driving

Warning Signs of Thermostat Failure

Engine Overheating

Temperature gauge in red zone, steam from hood, or coolant boiling over

Engine Runs Too Cold

Temperature gauge stays low, heater blows cold air, poor fuel economy

Fluctuating Temperature

Temperature gauge moves erratically between normal and hot

Check Engine Light

Codes related to engine temperature or coolant temperature sensor

Coolant Leaks

Leak near thermostat housing from failed gasket or housing crack
