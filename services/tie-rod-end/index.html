Tie Rod End Replacement

Ensure precise steering and safe alignment with new tie rod ends

What Are Tie Rod Ends?

Tie rod ends are critical steering components that connect your steering rack to the steering knuckles at each front wheel. They allow the wheels to move up and down with the suspension while maintaining steering control. When tie rod ends wear out, they create dangerous play in the steering system, affect wheel alignment, and can cause tire wear. In extreme cases, a failed tie rod end can cause complete loss of steering control.

Important: Alignment Required

After replacing tie rod ends, a professional wheel alignment is necessary to ensure proper toe settings and prevent rapid tire wear. We can set preliminary alignment to make the vehicle drivable, but a professional alignment at a shop with alignment equipment is required for proper completion of the repair.

Warning Signs of Worn Tie Rod Ends

Loose or Wandering Steering

Vehicle doesn't track straight, requires constant steering corrections, or feels vague

Clunking or Knocking Sounds

Noise when turning steering wheel or going over bumps, especially at low speeds

Vibration in Steering Wheel

Shaking or shimmy felt through steering wheel while driving

Uneven Tire Wear

Excessive wear on inside or outside edges of tires (toe wear)

Alignment Won't Hold

Vehicle pulls to one side even after recent alignment

Safety Warning

Do Not Ignore Worn Tie Rod Ends

Severely worn tie rod ends are a critical safety hazard. In extreme cases, a tie rod end can separate completely, causing immediate loss of steering control on that wheel.

If you hear clunking sounds or feel excessive play in the steering, have your vehicle inspected immediately.
